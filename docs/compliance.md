# EU compliance engineering annex

> **Engineering guidance, not legal advice.** This annex describes the controls and evidence present in this repository. It does not determine a lawful basis, controller or processor status, international-transfer mechanism, AI Act role, risk classification, retention period, or whether a deployment complies with law. The deploying organization must make those decisions with its data protection officer and qualified counsel before processing personal or regulated data.

This annex is a deployment worksheet, not a certification. Complete every `Deployment decision` cell for the exact organizations, contracts, cluster regions, model accounts, rooms, agents, integrations, and intended purposes in scope. Treat an empty cell as a release blocker, not as an implicit Fgentic default.

## Regulatory baseline and change control

This mapping was checked on **2026-07-18** against the official texts and guidance below:

- [GDPR, Regulation (EU) 2016/679](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2016/679/oj)
- [EDPB Guidelines 07/2020 on controller and processor concepts, final version](https://www.edpb.europa.eu/documents/guideline/guidelines-072020-on-the-concepts-of-controller-and-processor-in-the-gdpr_en)
- [European Commission rules on international data transfers](https://commission.europa.eu/law/law-topic/data-protection/international-dimension-data-protection/rules-international-data-transfers_en)
- [EU AI Act, Regulation (EU) 2024/1689](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj)
- [European Commission AI Act implementation page](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/regulatory-framework-ai)

The Commission implementation page reports a political agreement that would change parts of the high-risk-system timetable. Political agreement is not a substitute for the applicable legal text. Before a production decision, record the consolidated legislation, national rules, sector rules, Commission guidance, and effective dates counsel actually relied on.

## Decide roles before selecting controls

GDPR roles are functional: they follow who determines the purposes and essential means of each processing operation, not the label in a template. Installing open-source software does not make the Fgentic project the deployment's processor. A hosting, support, model, identity, or federation counterparty may have a different role for each operation; document the facts and contract rather than assigning one role platform-wide.

Use a separate record for each processing purpose:

| Required decision                                                         | Deployment decision |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------- |
| Purpose, data subjects, personal-data categories, and lawful basis        | _Required_          |
| Controller, any joint controllers, processors, and subprocessors          | _Required_          |
| Data locations, remote access locations, and transfer mechanism           | _Required_          |
| Retention period, start event, deletion owner, and legal-hold override    | _Required_          |
| Data-subject request intake, identity proof, response owner, and evidence | _Required_          |
| DPIA threshold and approved assessment, where applicable                  | _Required_          |
| Security, incident, breach-notification, and audit-access owners          | _Required_          |
| AI Act role, intended purpose, risk classification, and applicable date   | _Required_          |

For GDPR accountability, this worksheet supports—but does not replace—the controller's or processor's Article 30 record, Article 28 terms, Article 32 risk-based measures, Article 35 DPIA where required, and Chapter V transfer assessment.

## Retention and data-subject rights

The reference offers finite defaults and a store-by-store erasure runbook, not a universal schedule. Before go-live:

1. Set periods from purpose, necessity, legal duties, holds, partner commitments, and recovery needs; do not copy 90 days merely because it is the tracked Matrix default.
1. Test the complete [retention and erasure runbook](retention.md) on synthetic data, including MAS deactivation, Synapse redaction, media deletion, downstream review, backup expiry, and federated notices.
1. Record known residuals. Kagent lacks supported per-user purge; state events, metadata, caches, remote homeservers, providers, integrations, exports, and protected backups cannot be represented as erased by one local command.
1. Export authorized evidence before expiry or legal hold. A content-free audit record cannot reconstruct a purged instruction.
1. Reapply approved deletion after any restore that rehydrates an older copy.

## EU AI Act positioning

Fgentic is a composable collaboration and delegation platform, not a legal classification. The intended purpose and the way an organization places, provides, substantially modifies, or uses the assembled system determine its AI Act role and risk tier. A general agent assistant is not automatically high-risk; using it for an Annex III purpose such as employment, education, essential services, law enforcement, migration, or justice can change the analysis. Prohibited practices and sector rules must be screened separately.

The table maps engineering support to obligations that may apply. `Supports` never means `satisfies`.

### Evidence the platform can provide

- A Matrix sender, room/event, selected agent/version, durable A2A state, kagent context/task, terminal outcome, and Matrix reply can be joined when all required records exist.
- Kagent task metadata can contain per-task token usage; agentgateway and Prometheus provide provider/model aggregate corroboration.
- The opt-in regulated identity component has a content-bounded design, but D19 explicitly does not claim it is deployed.
- Public cross-organization delegation can return a signed, content-free usage receipt binding an authorized machine client, task, outcome, timestamp, request hash, and token reservation.

Use the exact claims and gaps in [Delegation Attribution Audit](audit.md). In particular, the chain does not prove a real person, authenticate `X-User-Id` at kagent, deterministically join an individual model request, preserve a purged prompt, or turn reserved tokens into consumed tokens or currency cost.

### Explicitly out of scope

This repository does not provide:

- legal role or high-risk classification;
- a lawful basis, privacy notice, record of processing, DPA, SCC package, transfer impact assessment, DPIA, or fundamental-rights impact assessment;
- prohibited-practice screening, sector authorization, conformity assessment, CE marking, EU database registration, or post-market legal process;
- proof that an external model account honors a claimed region, zero-retention setting, deletion request, or subprocessor list;
- a complete per-user purge across kagent, federation peers, clients, providers, integrations, exports, and backups;
- a six-month durable high-risk-system log, generic AI-output provenance marking, mandatory human approval workflow, or automated incident reporting.

## Pre-publication and go-live evidence

Keep this annex in draft status for a specific deployment until all applicable items are attached to an access-controlled review record:

1. Completed role, purpose, lawful-basis, country, recipient, retention, and deletion inventory.
1. Current contracts and subprocessor/transfer evidence for every external IdP, model, bridge, MCP, storage, support, and federation party.
1. Approved DPIA and AI Act role/risk assessment where required, including intended purpose and prohibited/high-risk screening.
1. Synthetic retention/erasure drill with residuals and backup/federation behavior recorded.
1. Exact attribution evidence and access/retention controls for every log relied upon.
1. User, worker, and partner disclosures rendered in the actual clients and languages in scope.
1. Human legal and DPO review recorded with date, scope, reviewer, findings, and approval or exceptions.

Repository checks prove links, formatting, manifests, and selected control contracts. They cannot replace legal review, contractual evidence, or a deployment drill.
